Many learners wait until they feel ready before they speak. The challenge is that confidence rarely appears on its own — it grows through practice.

## 1. Start with short, real phrases

You do not need to sound perfect. You need to sound clear. Pick three phrases you use in daily life — greetings, requests, plans — and say them out loud every day.

## 2. Record your voice for 2 minutes

Listen without judging yourself. Write down one thing that went well and one thing you want to improve. This habit helps you track real progress, not just feelings.

## 3. Choose conversation over perfection

In class we focus on useful mistakes: the ones that block communication. Everything else is part of natural learning.

> The goal is not to avoid mistakes. The goal is to express yourself freely.
